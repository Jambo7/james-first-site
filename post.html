5 ways to cut food waste with what you already have

March 1, 2025

Tips Food waste Habits

You don’t need a full pantry overhaul to waste less. Here are five simple habits that help you use what you’ve got before it goes bad—and how PantryPilot can support each one.

1. Check before you shop

Quickly scan what’s in the fridge and pantry before writing a list or hitting the store. When your inventory lives in one place, you’re less likely to buy duplicates or forget what’s already there.

2. Put “use first” items front and center

Whether it’s in the app or on a shelf, keep soon-to-expire items visible. PantryPilot’s expiry view and reminders make it easy to see what needs using so you can plan meals around those ingredients first.

3. Match recipes to your stock

Instead of picking a recipe and then shopping, start with what you have. Use recipe ideas that are filtered by your current inventory so you cook with existing ingredients and buy only the gaps.

4. Log leftovers

When you put away leftovers, add them to your inventory (e.g. “chicken curry, 2 portions”). You’ll get reminders to use them and can include them in recipe suggestions so they don’t get forgotten.

5. Do a weekly “clear the fridge” meal

Pick one meal a week that’s built from whatever needs using up—stir-fries, soups, and grain bowls work well. Your inventory and recipe suggestions can guide you so nothing gets left behind.

Small shifts like these add up. With PantryPilot keeping track of what you have and when it expires, you can focus on cooking and cutting waste without the mental load.
